Common Questions

Is the inspection really free?
Yes. We come out, assess the damage, and document it for your claim at no cost. There’s no obligation to use us for the repair.
Will filing a claim make my insurance rates go up?
Rates can change after a claim, but often storm and weather-related claims are treated differently than at-fault claims. We can’t guarantee your premium won’t change, but we can help you get what you’re owed so you’re not paying for covered damage out of pocket.
Do you work with my insurance company?
We work with all major carriers (State Farm, Allstate, USAA, Farmers, Liberty Mutual, and others). We provide the documentation they need and communicate with your adjuster so the process is smooth.
How long does the whole process take?
It depends on your carrier and the scope of damage. Typically we can inspect within a few days of your request. Once the claim is filed, approval and scheduling vary; we’ll give you a clearer timeline after the inspection.
What if I’ve already had an adjuster out?
You can still get a second opinion. We can review what was documented and help you understand if something was missed or underpaid — and work with you and your carrier to correct it if needed.
